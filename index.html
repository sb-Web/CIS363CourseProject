An Introduction to Programming

At its heart, programming is the way people direct computers and devices to carry out even the simplest operation. The start of programming goes all the way back to the 1800s, and Ada Lovelace is credited with creating the first program. The way directions are delivered has changed drastically from the early days, and has been made easier with the development of formal languages. Although progamming can become complex, even with modern code, there are far less mathematical algorithms to be figured manually and many languages used today come close to plain English.
