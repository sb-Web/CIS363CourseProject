An Introduction to Programming in SQL

SQL, or Structured Query Language, is a language designed to interact with relational databases. Relational databases store information (data) in tables, which are related to each other. For instance, a database may include two tables called "customers" and "orders". The two tables are related because a customer would initiate an order, and will likely have a common column (attribute) of customer_id to tie each customer to their order(s).
